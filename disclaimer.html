1. Regulatory Classification

PreStocks is an Educational & Analytical Platform

After careful review of SEC, FINRA, and state securities regulations, PreStocks is classified as follows:

CategoryClassificationRegulatory Implication
Primary Activity Financial education & market research tools Not subject to Investment Advisers Act registration
Paper Trading Simulation / educational exercise No broker-dealer registration required (no real transactions)
AI Research Automated data aggregation & analysis General commentary, not personalized advice (publisher's exclusion)
Risk Scores Algorithmic analytical tool Educational metric, not a suitability determination
Recommendations Content-based filtering (similar to "related articles") Not a recommendation to buy/sell within securities law meaning

Why We Are Not a Registered Investment Advisor

Under SEC guidance, an investment advisor provides specific advice about securities for compensation. PreStocks qualifies for the publisher's exclusion (Section 202(a)(11)(D) of the Investment Advisers Act) because:

  1. Our content is of general and regular circulation (available to all subscribers equally)
  2. We do not provide advice tailored to individual financial circumstances
  3. We do not manage client assets or have custody of funds
  4. Our AI generates general analysis, not personalized recommendations based on a user's specific portfolio needs

Monitoring & Re-evaluation

We continuously monitor regulatory developments. If our service evolves to include features that could constitute investment advice (e.g., personalized portfolio rebalancing, direct execution), we will seek appropriate registration before launching such features.

2. Required Disclosures

General Investment Risks

Investing in securities involves risk. You may lose some or all of your investment. Pre-IPO and private company investments are particularly risky:

  • Illiquidity: Private company shares may not be sellable for years
  • Information asymmetry: Less disclosure than public companies
  • Valuation uncertainty: No public market price; valuations are estimates
  • Dilution: Future funding rounds may dilute earlier investors
  • Total loss: Many startups fail; your investment may become worthless
  • Regulatory risk: Securities laws may restrict who can invest in private companies

Pre-IPO Specific Disclosures

  • Private company data is often self-reported and may not be audited
  • Valuations displayed are from funding rounds and may not reflect current fair market value
  • IPO timing and pricing are uncertain; companies may choose to stay private indefinitely
  • Secondary market prices (where available) can vary significantly from funding round valuations
  • Accredited investor requirements may apply to actual pre-IPO investments in your jurisdiction

Paper Trading Disclosures

  • Simulated results do not represent actual trading
  • Real execution would involve fees, spreads, market impact, and liquidity constraints not modeled
  • Psychological factors of risking real money are absent in simulation
  • Past simulated performance does not predict future real-world results

3. AI-Generated Content: Labeling & Limitations

How AI Content Is Labeled

All AI-generated content on PreStocks is clearly identified using:

  • A visible 🤖 AI Generated badge on every AI output
  • Confidence scores (where applicable) indicating the model's certainty
  • Source attribution — every fact links to its underlying data source
  • Timestamp — when the analysis was generated
  • Model identification — which AI system produced the output

Known Limitations of AI Analysis

LimitationImpactMitigation
Hallucination AI may state incorrect facts confidently Cross-reference with source data links; confidence scores flag uncertainty
Training data cutoff May not know very recent events Real-time data feeds supplement AI knowledge; timestamps shown
Bias in training data May over/underweight certain sectors or factors Regular bias audits; multiple model ensemble reduces single-model bias
Lack of causal reasoning Correlation ≠ causation in risk analysis Risk scores use multiple independent signals; methodology is documented
Context window limits May not consider all relevant information Analysis specifies which data sources were considered
No real-time awareness Cannot react to breaking news instantly Last-updated timestamps; real-time price data separate from AI analysis

Critical Reminder: AI-generated risk scores, research summaries, and portfolio suggestions are tools to inform your own research — not substitutes for professional financial advice. Always verify critical information independently before making real investment decisions.
